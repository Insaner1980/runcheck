No. runcheck uses only standard Android APIs (BatteryManager, PowerManager, ConnectivityManager, StorageManager) that are available without root. Some advanced metrics like exact cycle count may require Android 14+ or specific manufacturer support.

It depends on your device. runcheck shows confidence badges — Accurate, Estimated, or Unavailable — for every reading. On Pixel and Samsung devices with Android 14+, health data comes directly from hardware. On older devices, health is estimated with clear labeling.

Minimal impact. Background monitoring uses WorkManager with configurable intervals (15, 30, or 60 minutes). The app respects battery optimization and doesn't fight the OS. Real-time monitoring only runs when you're actively viewing the app.

Android 8.0 (Oreo) and above. Some features like trash management require Android 11+, and certain battery metrics are more accurate on Android 14+. The app adapts its capabilities to your device's API level automatically.

The score combines four categories: Battery (40%), Thermal (25%), Network (25%), and Storage (10%). Each sub-score factors in current state, trends, and device-specific reliability. The algorithm uses confidence-weighted readings — if a metric is unreliable, it contributes less to the final score rather than skewing it.
